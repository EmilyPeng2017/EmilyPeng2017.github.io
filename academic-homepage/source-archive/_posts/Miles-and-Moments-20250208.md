To be honest, I didn’t plan to run 15 km today. But as someone with a terrible sense of direction, I somehow ended up going the wrong way, and once I realized it, I had no choice but to keep going. So, 15 km it was! ORZ!

<!-- more -->
What started as a routine run turned into an unexpected adventure when I found myself in an unfamiliar area that looked like a cluster of factories. I'm sorry but I really like taking photos >_<. I couldn’t help but laugh at the absurdity of the situation. There I was, stopping every few minutes to snap pictures, feeling like a spy on a secret mission.

<div style="display: flex; justify-content: center; flex-wrap: wrap; gap: 20px; margin-bottom: 20px;"> <img src="/img/blogs/12_running/20250208_220858896_iOS.jpg" alt="Running Record" width="300" title="Running Record"> </div>
The weather wasn’t ideal. It was a bit chilly, and the skies were far from clear. But despite the less-than-perfect conditions, running always has a way of grounding me. It’s my go-to remedy for clearing my mind and finding my balance again.
