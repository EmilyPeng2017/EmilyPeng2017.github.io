# CMOS Integrated Circuits: A versatile platform for affordable, low-power biomedical devices 

**Presenter:** Prof. Constantine Sideris, Assistant Professor of Electrical Engineering at Stanford University
**Assistant Professor, Electrical Engineering, Stanford University, Stanford University**

<!-- more -->

> Introduction of the speaker: Prof. Constantine Sideris is an Assistant Professor of Electrical Engineering at Stanford University. He received his Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from the University of California, Berkeley, where he worked on low-power integrated circuits for biomedical applications. His research focuses on the design and implementation of CMOS integrated circuits for wearable and implantable biomedical devices, with an emphasis on energy efficiency, miniaturization, and biocompatibility.


## integrated circuits for medicine
- sensing
- processing
- wireless communication
- power management
