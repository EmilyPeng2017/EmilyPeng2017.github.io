üß™ What is a Solvent?

A solvent is a substance that dissolves a solute, forming a solution. Solvents are widely used in chemical synthesis, industrial applications, and scientific research. A good solvent should have:

  • High solubility for the intended solute.
  • Volatility for easy removal after use.
  • Chemical stability under reaction conditions.
  • Low toxicity (but in reality, most common solvents are hazardous).

The toxicity of solvents often arises from their chemical properties [1]:

  • Volatility & Inhalation Risk ‚Äì Many solvents evaporate quickly, making it easy to inhale toxic vapors (e.g., benzene, n-hexane).
  • Lipid Solubility & Bioaccumulation ‚Äì Solvents like to dissolve in fat tissues, making them accumulate in the nervous system and organs.
  • Metabolic Byproducts ‚Äì Some solvents (e.g., methanol) are metabolized into more toxic substances in the body.
  • Neurotoxicity ‚Äì Long-term exposure can damage the nervous system, leading to symptoms like dizziness, tremors, and neuropathy.
  • Carcinogenicity ‚Äì Some solvents (e.g., benzene) are known to cause cancer with prolonged exposure.
